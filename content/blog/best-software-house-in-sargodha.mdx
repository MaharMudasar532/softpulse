## What a Software House Does — and Does Not Do

A **software house** designs, builds, and maintains digital products: websites, web applications, mobile apps, APIs, e-commerce stores, and internal tools. A credible team handles discovery, UI/UX, development, testing, deployment, and post-launch support.

What they typically do not do — unless explicitly scoped — is guarantee viral growth, run your paid ads indefinitely for free, or build unlimited features for a fixed low fee without trade-offs.

Clarity on scope prevents the disappointment that gives local IT companies a bad name.

## How to Evaluate the Best Software House in Sargodha

"Best" is not a trophy — it is fit for your project. Use this checklist before signing a contract.

### Portfolio and live products

Ask to see deployed work — live URLs, App Store listings, admin demos. Mockups are not proof. At Soft Pulse, our [services](/services) span web, mobile, and e-commerce because we ship maintained products, not one-off templates.

### Technical depth

- Who architects the backend?
- Which frameworks are used — and why?
- How is security handled — authentication, data validation, HTTPS, backups?
- Is code version-controlled and documented?

### Communication rhythm

Weekly updates, staging environments, and ticket systems beat vague "it will be done soon" messages. Good teams explain delays early.

### Post-launch support

Bugs appear after launch. Hosting, monitoring, and maintenance should be discussed upfront — not after you are locked in.

### Training and talent pipeline

## Red Flags When Choosing a Developer

Walk away or dig deeper if you notice:

- **No discovery phase** — Quotes before understanding requirements
- **100% upfront payment** with no milestones
- **Copy-paste portfolios** — Same demo site for every client
- **No mention of testing** — "We will fix bugs after launch" without a plan
- **Offshore bait-and-switch** — Sales locally, unknown freelancers abroad without oversight
- **Guaranteed rankings or revenue** — SEO and app success depend on many factors

The **best software house in Sargodha** will ask hard questions about your users, budget, and timeline — because they intend to deliver, not just collect a deposit.

## Questions to Ask on Your First Call

1. Can you show a similar project deployed in production?
2. Who owns the source code after payment?
3. What is your testing process before handoff?
4. How do you handle scope changes?
5. What hosting and security practices do you recommend?
6. Do you offer maintenance retainers?

Strong answers build trust. Evasiveness is data.
